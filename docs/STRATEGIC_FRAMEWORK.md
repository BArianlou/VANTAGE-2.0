# VANTAGE 2.0 | Strategic Framework
**Document Version:** 1.1.0  
**Classification:** Proprietary / Physics-Native Architecture

## 1. The Phase Space Methodology
Vantage 2.0 rejects standard statistical linear regression. Instead, it models systems within a **Phase Space** defined by three primary vectors:

* **Fuel ($F$):** The kinetic potential and liquidity available to the system.
* **Entropy ($S$):** The measure of structural friction and systemic debt.
* **Maturity ($M$):** The temporal scaling factor. $M$ determines the "Inertia" of the manifold; high-maturity systems require greater force to trigger regime transitions.

## 2. Regime Boundary Logic
The engine identifies four distinct quadrants of systemic health. Transition velocity between these states is governed by the $F/S$ ratio relative to the $M$ coefficient.

| Quadrant | State | Vector Dynamics | Potential Well Depth |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Q1: Melt-Up** | High Luminosity | $+F, -S$ | Shallow (Low Persistence) |
| **Q2: Bull** | High Energy | $+F, +S$ | Moderate (Stable Growth) |
| **Q3: Stall** | Structural Friction | $-F, +S$ | Deep (Metastable Trap) |
| **Q4: Crash** | High Density Singularity | $-F, ++S$ | Extreme (Collapse Flow) |

## 3. Architectural Layers
The VANTAGE 2.0 ecosystem is deployed across three distinct logic substrates:
1. **[Normalization Substrate](./NORMALIZATION_SUBSTRATE.md):** The translation layer converting raw signals into geometry-compatible coordinates.
2. **[Predictive Manifold](./PREDICTIVE_MANIFOLD.md):** The geometric engine managing metric tensors, geodesics, and curvature flow.
3. **[Regime Boundaries](./REGIME_BOUNDARIES.md):** The formal ontology governing state transitions and instability triggers.

## 4. The Instability Trigger ($\vec{a}_s$)
Predictive accuracy is achieved by monitoring the **Acceleration of Entropy**. When the rate of change in system disorder outpaces the available fuel, a "Phase Transition" (Regime Shift) is triggered:
$$\vec{a}_s > \gamma s$$
