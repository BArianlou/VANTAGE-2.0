# VANTAGE 2.0
Regime‑Aware Predictive Intelligence Engine

## Executive Summary
Vantage 2.0 is a regime‑aware intelligence engine that models complex environments through entropy, momentum, and structural drift. It treats systems as evolving fields, identifies instability before it becomes observable, and generates predictive trajectories grounded in physics‑native dynamics rather than statistical assumptions.

## Core Architecture

### Dual‑Axis Modeling
Vantage 2.0 operates on two orthogonal axes:
- Entropy Axis: measures disorder, dispersion, and systemic uncertainty.
- Momentum Axis: captures directional pressure, persistence, and kinetic bias.

### Regime Detection
Identifies volatility‑driven regime shifts, structural drift, phase transitions, and breakdowns in signal coherence. Regimes are treated as physical states with boundaries.

### State‑Transition Dynamics
Models transitions as entropy‑driven expansions or contractions, momentum‑driven accelerations or reversals, and hybrid states where drift and volatility interact.

### Metadata Coherence Layer
Normalizes heterogeneous inputs into a unified structure that preserves dimensional consistency, temporal alignment, and lifecycle visibility.

### Standardized Normalization (F, S, M)
All signals are mapped to the closed interval [0, 1] to maintain manifold stability, prevent derivative jitter, and ensure interpretable activation of instability triggers across substrates.

## System Capabilities

- Regime Classification  
  Identifies the active system regime and its stability characteristics.

- Structural Drift Mapping  
  Detects slow, cumulative deformation in system behavior.

- Predictive Trajectory Generation  
  Produces forward‑looking paths based on entropy‑momentum interactions.

- Volatility State Analysis  
  Measures how uncertainty propagates through the system.

- Scenario and Stress Modeling  
  Evaluates system behavior under perturbation.

- Dependency and Influence Mapping  
  Reveals how variables interact across regimes.

- Decision‑Support Visualizations  
  Highlights pressure zones, risk surfaces, and directional bias.

## Conceptual Model

Vantage 2.0 is grounded in physics‑native principles:

- Entropy: quantifies disorder and uncertainty.
- Momentum: captures directional persistence and kinetic bias.
- Drift: represents slow structural deformation in system behavior.
- Regime Boundaries: define stable versus unstable operational states.
- Phase Transitions: mark discontinuities where system behavior changes class.

The engine treats all environments as evolving fields rather than static datasets.
